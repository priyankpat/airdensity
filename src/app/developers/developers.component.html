Why Density Altitude?

This video will give you a good idea of the dangers this app seeks to mitigate.

I'd like to explain the purpose of this app to developers, which is readily apparent to skydivers, but probably not to those in the tech industry.

Obviously, many skydivers are adrenaline junkies. A skydive lasts about 4 minutes, costs $25, and as such, skydivers want to squeeze all the fun they can out of it.

In our first jumps, we're advised not to make low turns, as this causes the parachute to dive at the ground... But the experts know that the perfect low turn generates lots of speed for the landing, resulting in a surf across the grass that can't be explained with words.

Of course... this can go terribly wrong. The parachute must come out of the dive at the right moment, or you can hit the ground with lethal force.

This app provides a metric, Density Altitude, which represents how thick or thin the air is. As the air density changes from day to day and even throughout the day, the same dive can recover too quickly... or too late, resulting in injury or death.

By making this metric accessible, skydivers are better informed to make these high performance landings in a more calculated way, without relying too much on feel.

How the App works

This app was built with Angular JS. It makes an API call, takes data from the JSON, stores those in variables, and crunches the numbers in a formula to get Density Altitude. The DOM is modified by Angular directives, and the results come out on the home page. The numbers update by the minute, providing up to date data (many aviation data sources update every 4-6 hours). I believe this app is the best, one-tap result available to skydivers today.
